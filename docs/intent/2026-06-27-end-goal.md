# End goal — Lanto ICT autonomous trading system

> Confirmed intent, captured via `/interview-me` on 2026-06-27. This is the durable
> north star both the user and Claude work against. It defines what "done" means for
> the whole project. Downstream artifacts (specs, plans, tasks) consume this.

## One sentence

A personal, fully-autonomous bot that trades Lanto's 3-pillar ICT method on the
user's real Tradovate micro-index account — unattended, faithful to Lanto, with
trust earned through exact backtest≡live parity.

## The goal, line by line

- **Outcome:** A fully-autonomous bot that trades Lanto's 3-pillar ICT method on the
  user's **real Tradovate** micro-index account (MNQ/MES, SMT-selected per day) across
  the **London + NY** killzones, **unattended**, pulling its own triggers with always-on
  guardrails (valid stop · sizing within limits · daily-loss halt).
- **User:** Just the user. Personal money machine — **not** a product, not multi-user.
- **Correctness =** trades **the way Lanto would** (matches his actual setups / Discord
  calls — faithful to the method) **first**. Profit is the expected *consequence* of
  fidelity, never an override. A faithful-but-losing behavior is not "fixed" by
  protecting P&L.
- **Decision path:** 100% **deterministic** walker chain. **Zero LLM in the trade
  path** — it must place correct trades with every LLM down. The LLM (Claude/Codex) is
  narration / journaling / brief-commentary only; it never decides or vetoes a trade.
- **Trust mechanism (the keystone):** **backtest ≡ live parity.** Identical setups,
  entries, stops, targets, and trades — differing **only** in fill price (small live
  slippage is fine). Same brain folded both ways. The backtest is the crystal ball;
  live is guaranteed to reproduce it.
- **Real-money gate:** flip from demo to real money when the **backtest** shows
  faithful, **net-positive** results over a **window the user trusts** (representative —
  not the cherry-picked +138R weeks; mindful that the edge is regime-dependent and
  early-year OOS ran negative). Live can't drift from backtest — that's the
  non-negotiable.
- **UI mandate (transparency, not aesthetics):** every panel reads the **same analysis
  the bot reads**. It shows **what the system is thinking and why**, as clearly for the
  user as for the bot. One faithful source of truth — **no UI-only or fabricated
  numbers**. Priority is fidelity of what's shown, not polish.
- **Supervision:** the user monitors and hot-fixes **during bring-up only**. "Done" is
  **hands-off** during sessions, review after.
- **First live demo session:** the next **London**.

## Out of scope (explicit non-goals)

- Selling, sharing, or productizing it; multi-account / multi-user support.
- Any LLM **deciding or vetoing** trades (LLM stays out of the trade path).
- Chasing P&L **over** fidelity to Lanto.
- A polished UI that isn't backed by the bot's **real** decision data.
- A fixed P&L / win-rate / session-count bar as the real-money gate (the gate is
  backtest results + parity, judged by the user).

## How trust is earned (the chain of guarantees)

1. The deterministic walker chain is the **only** setup producer.
2. Backtest and live fold the **same** truth function over the **same** inputs →
   identical decisions (parity invariant, enforced by tests + tapes).
3. Therefore a backtest the user trusts ⇒ confidence that live behaves the same.
4. The UI surfaces the bot's **actual** inputs/reasoning ⇒ the user can read the same
   truth the bot acts on, in real time and in review.
5. Real money is armed only after (3) holds on a trusted window and (4) is faithful.

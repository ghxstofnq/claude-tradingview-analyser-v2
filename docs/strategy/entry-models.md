# Entry Models (Pillar 3)

Three distinct entry models: **MSS (reversal)**, **Trend (continuation)**, and
**Inversion (failed opposing PD array)**. Each is a structured way to put risk on; all
three are triggered by a 1-minute candle-close confirmation (see
[`confirmation.md`](confirmation.md)). Sources: *How I Enter The Market / Entry Models*
(2/9/2026, `MoFNCTq9aXs`) and *How I Trade The Stock Market 2024* (`TGIUjVBBemo`).

> "These are one of our three components. Don't blindly take trades off this alone —
> draw/bias and price action have to align too." (ENTRY ~00:00)

---

## Best-gap selection (block out the noise)

Before any model, pick the **best** gap. Two components, in order:

1. **Displacement** — a large body with minimal wickage. A bigger gap is a bigger
   inefficiency the market is motivated to fill.
2. **Took liquidity while forming** — as the gap printed, it swept an internal high /
   low / swing point. Liquidity shifted into the gap.

> "Two components: displacement — the body of the gap is large, minimal wicks — and a
> gap which takes liquidity while forming, taking out an internal high or low as it
> displaces." (ENTRY ~05:38–07:30)

On a busy 1m/5m chart there may be ten gaps in a 30-minute window — these two filters
pick the one that matters.

---

## MSS model — reversal after a liquidity grab

**Purpose:** catch a reversal at a session high/low or HTF POI after stops are taken and
structure shifts. (Long described below; invert for a short.)

**§1 — Context & draw.** A clear draw is near completion (HTF sell-side taken, or price
trading into a large 4H/Daily bullish FVG). You expect a reaction once the final
liquidity is cleared.

**§2 — Liquidity grab.** Price runs **below an obvious, significant low** — Asia low,
London low, prior-day low, or a very clear intraday swing low — taking stops, often
leaving a long lower wick. *The grab must be significant:* never an MSS off a 1m
equal-low. Read the sell sequence into the low — a hard, then stagnant sell is low
probability; a significant low with a clean reversal is high probability.

> "Always look at the type of sell sequence into the low… I never play an MSS off a 1m
> pair of equal lows, especially with a very bearish open." (BIAS ~29:43–31:34)

**§3 — Market structure shift with displacement.** After the sweep, price reverses
**sharply up**, breaks the most recent **lower high**, and does it with **displacement**
— a wide-range candle/sequence leaving an FVG, at a speed that **matches or exceeds** the
down-move.

> "You want price on the way back up to displace at the same speed it came down, if not
> more — sellers step out, buyers step in." (ENTRY ~08:26–09:21; BIAS ~28:47)

**§4 — Retrace to the FVG (without a new low).** The displacement leg leaves a bullish
FVG; price pulls back into it (ideally the CE / midpoint) **without making a new low**.
If price instead closes back through the grabbed level, the reversal premise is dead.

**§5 — Entry confirmation (1m).** On the retest, wait for a **bullish 1m candle close**
that holds within/above the FVG and pushes away with a clean body. No trade if price
wicks through and closes weak, or chops in the FVG for >10–15 minutes.

**§6 — Risk & target.** Stop: a few ticks below the **MSS low** (or below the FVG low) —
structural invalidation. Target: next internal high, then session high / HTF buy-side
draw.

**A+ MSS example.** Daily trades into a large bullish 4H FVG that swept a prior weekly
low (draw to buy-side above). London presses down and takes Asia low + yesterday's low
in one fast push. A strong bullish displacement candle then breaks the last 5m lower
high, leaving a clean 5m FVG. At NY open price retraces into it; on 1m a quick tap then a
full-body bullish close back above the midpoint with almost no lower wick. Enter on that
close, stop a few ticks below the MSS low, TP1 the last internal high, TP2 London high,
runner toward prior-day high. *A+ because draw, sweep, MSS, FVG, and confirmation all
align.*

## Trend model — continuation in the direction of displacement

**Purpose:** join an already-established move after the MSS is done and a clean trend is
in play. Take only off **immense conviction** — price already trading strongly in one
direction.

**§1 — Context & HTF bias.** HTF (Daily/4H) is clearly moving, leaving bullish FVGs that
are repeatedly respected; an earlier MSS to the upside is done — you are now in the
continuation phase.

**§2 — Strong impulse leg.** On 5m/1m price makes a strong impulse up (wide bodies,
little overlap), leaving one or more fresh bullish FVGs.

**§3 — Pullback into the internal FVG.** Price pulls back **within** the bullish
structure (still higher highs / higher lows) into an internal bullish FVG in the trend
direction. *A tap is enough — you do not need a full fill or CE fill.*

> "I don't mind price just touching the entry. I actually prefer price tapping the entry
> of the FVG and then coming right back up." (ENTRY ~11:15–12:11)

**§4 — Entry confirmation (1m).** A **bullish 1m close** with a strong body (stronger
than the pullback candles), closing away from the zone. No trade if price chops in the
FVG, or if structure breaks down (no longer higher lows).

**§5 — Risk & target.** Stop: below the **swing low that touches the FVG** (the deepest
point of the pullback, not just the confirmation wick) or below the FVG low. Target: next
internal high, then the HTF draw (e.g. prior daily high / 4H buy-side).

**A+ Trend example.** 4H is in a sustained up-move respecting prior bullish FVGs. London
made a big displacement leg up, MSS to the upside, leaving two bullish FVGs; NY opens
above them. On 5m price rallies to new highs leaving a fresh 5m bullish FVG, then
retraces into it with small orderly red candles. On 1m, a small bottoming wick then a
strong bullish close above the FVG CE. Buy on that close, stop below the local swing
low/FVG, TP1 the pullback high, TP2 the next HTF buy-side. *A+ because trend, HTF FVG
respect, clean impulse, clean pullback, decisive confirmation all align.*

## Inversion model — failed opposing PD array

**Purpose:** trade the **failure** of an opposing PD array as confirmation of your bias.
A bearish FVG failing in a bullish environment becomes a bullish inversion FVG (and vice
versa). Used when price is **so bullish it doesn't retrace** for a clean FVG entry.

**§1 — Context & HTF bias.** HTF is clearly bullish — strong upward displacement,
buy-side targets above, prior bearish PD arrays being consumed. You already expect higher
prices.

**§2 — Opposing FVG forms.** Within the up-move a small **bearish FVG** prints (a
counter-trend imbalance during a brief sell-off).

**§3 — Violation.** Instead of respecting it, price **blasts through** and a candle
**closes above the top** of that bearish FVG with displacement — turning the failed
bearish FVG into a **bullish inversion FVG**.

**§4 — Optional retest & confirmation.** *Conservative:* wait for price to pull back into
the inversion zone (now support) and a 1m bullish close from it. *Aggressive:* enter on
the close that violated the FVG, if context and speed are exceptional — the violating
close **is** the confirmation.

> "Some instances price is so bullish it doesn't feel like it needs to rebalance — it
> just goes. So you take the inversion: a candle close that invalidates the bearish FVG."
> (TRADE24 ~19:57–20:57)

**§5 — Risk & target.** Stop: below the **candle that closed through** the FVG, or below
the inversion FVG low / failed-leg extreme. Target: next buy-side liquidity (session
high, PDH, HTF high).

**A+ Inversion example.** 4H bullish FVGs respected for days; price approaching a prior
weekly high (main draw). During NY, after a strong rally, 5m prints a small bearish FVG;
a large green candle then rips straight through and closes well above it with no
rejection. On 1m price pulls back into the inversion zone and prints a full-body bullish
close. Buy on that confirmation, stop under the inversion low, TP first at the intraday
high then the weekly high. *A+ because a counter-trend PD array fails exactly in the
direction of HTF bias.*

## Multi-alignment (advanced) entry

The strongest entries stack **two imbalances making one move** — e.g. a **5m FVG
rebalance** that lines up with a **1m bearish-FVG go-invert** in the same spot. Two PD
arrays confirming together = a stronger A+.

> "Today's trade: a 5-minute bullish FVG rebalance paired with a 1-minute bearish FVG go
> bullish-invert, in one. Two imbalances to make a bullish move — that's why it was A+."
> (ENTRY ~25:13–27:05)

Treat this as a confluence amplifier on top of the three base models, not a fourth model.

---

## 1m vs 5m gap preference

Confirmation is always **1-minute** (see [`confirmation.md`](confirmation.md)). For the
gap itself, when forced to choose, prefer the **5-minute** gap — less noise; a clean 5m
gap is unambiguous, where 1m often shows several competing gaps.

> "I prefer the 5 — it just has less noise. A clear 5m gap isn't even debatable; on the
> 1m you'll have multiple good gaps." (ENTRY ~32:21–33:23)
